  • Dec. 21, 1988
    Pan American flight 103, a Boeing 747, took off from London, bound for New York City. It exploded over the town of Locerkbie; 270 people from 21 countries were killed, including all 259 passengers and crewmembers plus 11 people on the ground in Lockerbie.
  • Dec. 28, 1988
    British investigators concluded that a bomb in the luggage compartment caused the midair disentegration of plane.
  • Dec. 29, 1988
    The Federal Aviation Administration imposed new security measures on American Airlines that fly out of Pan Am airports in Western Europe and the Middle East.
  • Dec. 29, 1988
    After collecting thousands of pieces of debris, the Air Accidents Investigation Branch of the British Department of Transport determined that residue on the fragments was consistent with high-performance plastic explosive.
  • Jan. 1, 1989
    Seven hundred mourners, joined by British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, gathered at at Dryfesdale Church in Lockerbie for a memorial for the victims. About 200 American relatives and 100 Pan Am World Employees were in attendance.
  • Jan. 18, 1989
    An interfaith memorial was held at the Carrier Dome, with apporximately 15,000 people in attendance SU Chancellor Melvin A. Eggers announces a Rememberance Scholarship fund; the Daily Orange - the student-run newspaper at SU - released a special edition dedicated to the 35 student victims; the SU drama department performed a special tribute to the victims.
  • Jan. 21, 1989
    SU ROTCH held its own memorial for Timothy Michael Cardwell, an Army ROTCH Scholarship winner, a volunteer in the ROTC Ranger program and a sergeant in the 403rd Army Reserve National Guard at Syracuse. Almost 60 ROTC members, along with the Hendricks Chapel Choir, attended the service.
  • Jan. 31, 1989
    A single casket was buried in Dryfesdale Cemetery to represent the 17 victims whose bodies weren't found during the searches following the crash.
  • Feb. 3, 1989
    Inacurate reports - but thought to be acurate at the time - said western intelligence came to the conclusion that the bombing was commissioned by Iran and carried out by the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine-General Command with support from Syria and Libya.
  • March 16, 1989
    SU Chancellor Melvin A. Eggers visits Lockerbie; lays a wreath at the Grave of Missing Persons.
  • April 3, 1989
    Relatives and friends of the victims hold a demonstration and memorial vigil in Washington, D.C.
