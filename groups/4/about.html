December 21, 1988

25 years ago, Pan Am Flight 103 exploded over Lockerbie, Scotland, killing 270 people. 35 of the victims were students returning from Syracuse University's study abroad program. Every year, SU comes together during Remembrance Week to remember those young individuals, to keep them in our hearts, and to educate in order to keep tragedies like Pan Am 103 from happening again.

This year, in conjunction with the Newshouse, Jon Glass and Dan Pacheco's ICC 505 class set out to cover the 25th Remembrance Scholars, students selected each year to represent those lost 25 years ago. These are among SU's most promising students, just as the victims of Pan Am 103 were among the most promising students of their day. They have shown great dedication and consideration for the families of the victims, and through them, we honor the past and look to the future.
