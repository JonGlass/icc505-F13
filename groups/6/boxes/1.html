The Tragedy

On Dec. 21, 1988, Pan Am Flight 103, a flight traveling from the London Heathrow Airport in London to the John F. Kennedy Airport in Queens, exploded over the town of Lockerbie in Scotland 38 minutes after taking off. The bomb killed all 259 people aboard as well as 11 people on the ground.

On Nov. 13, 1991, Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed al-Megrahi and Al Amin Khalifa Fhimah of Libya were indicted by American and British investigators on 270 counts of murder, conspiracy to commit murder, and breaching Britain's 1982 Aviation Security Act.
