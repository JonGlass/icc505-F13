On Dec. 21, 1988, Pan Am Flight 103, a flight traveling from the London Heathrow Airport in London to the John F. Kennedy Airport in Queens, exploded over the town of Lockerbie in Scotland only 38 minutes after taking off from London. The bomb aboard the flight killed all 259 people aboard the Boeing 747 plane as well as the eleven people who were on the ground.

On Nov. 13, 1991, Abdelbaset Ali Mohmed al-Megrahi and Al Amin Khalifa Fhimah of Libya are indicted by American and British investigators on 270 counts of murder, conspiracy to commit murder and breaching Britain's 1982 Aviation Security Act.
