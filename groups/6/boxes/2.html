Syracuse University

Following the tragedy, SU developed the Remembrance Scholarship as well as Remembrance Week to honor the 270 victims, especially the 35 victims who were students at SU studying abroad in London.

The Remembrance Scholarship is granted to seniors who have demonsrated citizenship and have served the community. The program seeks to educate the campus community about terrorism by honoring and celebrating the 35 SU student victims and all 270 victims in general, connecting SU's experience following the tragedy and current events. The scholars receive $5,000.

Remembrance Week is a weeklong series of events in which the 270 victims, including the 35 SU students were who were studying abroad in London, are honored. This year's series, which ran from which marks the 25th anniversary of the tragedy, included portraits of the 35 SU student victims on display in the Panasci Lounge of the Schine Student Center, an exhibition of 35 empty chairs on the Kenneth A. Shaw Quad, a virtual timeline created by the Pan Am Flight 103/Lockerbie Air Disaster Archives, candlelight vigil and more.
