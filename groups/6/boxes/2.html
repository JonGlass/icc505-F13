Following the tragedy, Syracuse University developed its Remembrance Scholarship as well as Remembrance Week to honor the 270 victims, especially the 35 victims who were students at SU studying abroad in London.

The Remembrance Scholarship is granted to seniors who have demonsrated citizenship and have served the community. The goal of the program is to educate the campus community about terrorism by making a connection between the experience of Syracuse University in light of the Pan Am 103 tragedy and current events. The idea is to honor, remember and celebrate the 35 SU students who were on that flight and all 270 victims in general. The 35 scholars receive $5,000.

Source: Remembrance Scholar Website

Remembrance Week is a weeklong series of events in which the 270 victims, including the 35 SU students were who were studying abroad in London, are honored. This year's series, which marks the 25th anniversary of the tragedy, included portraits of the 35 SU student victims on display in the Panasci Lounge of the Schine Student Center, an exhibition of 35 empty chairs on the Kenneth A. Shaw Quad, a virtual timeline created by the Pan Am Flight 103/Lockerbie Air Disaster Archives, candlelight vigil and more.

Source: Syracuse University, Remembrance Scholar Website
