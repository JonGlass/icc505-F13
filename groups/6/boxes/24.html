Peace

The Common Prayer for Peace, composed by Hendricks Chapel dean Tiffany Steinwert and the Rev. Sandy Stoddart, minister of the Lockerbie (Dryfesdale) Church of Scotland is a 142-word passage rooted in the union of Lockerbie and SU, two separate communities that became intertwined in light of the Pan Am 103 tragedy. The prayer will be recited across the globe on Dec. 21, 2013, at 2:04 p.m. Eastern Time. The prayer's message follows:

We gather as a global community, uniquely woven together by our persistent pursuit of hope. Rising up from the ashes of tragedy, we proclaim our commitment to creating a better, more just world. In remembering our past, may we ignite a fierce passion for the future that propels us beyond boundaries of division and unites us in the common pursuit of life's most precious gifts: faith, hope and love. Today, in the shadow of the longest night of the year, we confidently declare that love and life shall always triumph over fear and violence. Help us to know that the light of love can never be extinguished as long as we tend to it by acts of compassion, justice, peace and reconciliation. Encourage us that by joining together we can create a living legacy through our actions in the world.
